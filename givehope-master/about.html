About SevaSahayog

About Us

Seva Sahayog Foundation has been working relentlessly since 2009 towards the development and welfare of the underprivileged sections of society. We started our informal operations in 2005, and got registered as a Not-for-Profit company under Section 25B (now Section 8) of the Companies Act 1956.

Seva Sahayog Foundation aims to engage socially conscious individuals, groups, and corporates with interventions that match their interests. This noble collaboration collectively adds value to society at large.

After working with the underprivileged sections of society for over a decade, we’ve recognized the gaps and areas for improvement at individual, institutional and community levels. Our competent staff and volunteers at the grassroots and in-office have maneuvered a reliable mechanism of their work and taken it to the next level.

We observed that corporates and individuals have a purpose they wish to support but lack resources that connect them to their preferred beneficiaries. We act as a bridge between the two and match them to interventions that are closer to their heart.

Our Story – The Beginning

What we do

To identify needy segments and connect them with Corporate Houses for social transformation.

Planning and implementing projects with our ground-level and in-office workforce. 

Promote social awareness and provide people a platform to contribute towards the betterment of society through volunteering opportunities.

Raising funds through Systematic Donation Plan, CSR funding, and personal donations.

Ensure impact oriented interventions at the grassroots level

Our Mission

To build a network of socially conscious people within India and around the world which will be a pool of resources with financial strength, knowledge, commitment and an aim to impact 3 million lives by 2030.

Our Accomplishment

Seva Sahayog Foundation has been predominantly working on Education, Empowerment, and Environment sectors at individual, Institutional, and Community levels

Our Network

Today we have an outreach of 600 thousand plus beneficiaries spread across 27 districts of Maharashtra with a strong engagement in 700 schools, and 190 communities.
